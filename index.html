What Is Inland Marine Insurance for Contractors?

Inland marine insurance is a specialized commercial property policy that protects the tools, equipment, trailers, and other movable business property contractors rely on every day. Despite the nautical-sounding name, modern inland marine coverage has nothing to do with boats — it earned the label from a 19th-century carve-out for cargo moving inland from the docks. Today it is the standard way contractors insure anything that does not stay bolted to one address.

Most general liability and commercial property policies stop protecting your equipment the moment it leaves your shop. A miter saw stolen out of a locked work van overnight, a generator destroyed when a jobsite trailer is broken into, a $7,000 laser level dropped off a roof — none of those are typically covered by a standard BOP. An inland marine policy (sometimes called a contractor's tool floater or equipment floater) fills that exact gap.

For Arizona-based contractors and trade businesses operating anywhere in the United States, Contractors Choice Agency writes inland marine policies that follow your gear: at the jobsite, in transit between jobs, sitting in a locked vehicle overnight, or stored in a third-party yard. Coverage can be sized for a single five-tool electrician kit or a multi-trailer roofing operation — and quotes are typically returned within 15 minutes once we see photos of your equipment.

  • Newly acquired equipment — automatic interim coverage for items added mid-policy

Frequently Asked Questions

What is inland marine insurance for contractors?

Inland marine insurance is a commercial property policy that protects tools, equipment, trailers, and other movable business property while it is being transported, stored off-site, or used away from your fixed business location. For contractors, it fills the coverage gap that standard general liability and commercial property policies leave open for mobile equipment.

What does a contractor tool & equipment policy cover?

Most policies cover theft, fire, vandalism, and accidental damage to power tools, hand tools, ladders, generators, compressors, welders, trailers, and leased or borrowed equipment. Coverage typically follows the equipment anywhere in the United States — at the jobsite, in transit, or stored in a truck overnight.

Do I need to schedule each tool individually?

It depends on item value. Most carriers require individual scheduling (with serial number and value) for items above a per-item threshold — typically $1,000 to $2,500. Smaller items are usually covered under an unscheduled blanket limit, often $5,000 to $25,000 in total.

Is rented or borrowed equipment covered?

Yes — most inland marine policies include a sub-limit for rented, leased, or borrowed equipment, which satisfies the insurance requirement most rental yards put on their contracts. Let us know your typical rental needs and we will size the limit accordingly.
